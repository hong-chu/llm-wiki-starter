# Workshop pre-work

Hey — before the workshop, please do these three things (~10 min). It
ensures we can spend our time on the interesting parts together and
that you arrive ready to play.

## What you'll do

1. **Install tools** (5 min)
   - [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) — free, local, no account required.
   - An agent of your choice:
     [Claude Code](https://docs.claude.com/en/docs/claude-code/overview)
     or [Codex CLI](https://github.com/openai/codex).

2. **Clone this template** (1 min)
   ```bash
   git clone https://github.com/hong-chu/llm-wiki-starter.git my-wiki
   cd my-wiki
   ```
   Open the folder in Obsidian: *Open folder as vault* → select
   `my-wiki/`. You'll see the empty scaffold.

3. **Pick a topic + collect 2–3 sources to bring** (5 min)

   Choose a topic narrow enough that 5–10 sources could meaningfully
   cover it. Some directions:
   - A research area (e.g. *Andrej Karpathy's AutoResearch*, *harness engineering*, *AI-agent OpenClaw vs. Hermes*).
   - Your team's runbooks, alert playbooks, or domain knowledge.
   - A hobby with a literature.
   - A specific technical sub-domain at work.

   Collect 2–3 starter sources somewhere handy (a folder, a notes
   file, a few links) — papers, blog posts, transcripts, screenshots,
   your own notes. **Don't drop them into `sources/` yet.** We'll do
   the first ingest together during the workshop; that's when the
   pattern clicks, and you'll get more out of seeing it for the first
   time with the group.

## What to bring to the workshop

- Your laptop with Obsidian + the agent installed and the repo cloned.
- A topic in mind + 2–3 sources ready to drop in.
- An answer to this question, in your head:

  > *Where in your current work do you (or someone on your team)
  > re-derive the same answer repeatedly, or where new sources
  > accumulate but nobody synthesizes them? Bring one example.*

We'll workshop your example live.

## If you get stuck

- Setup question? DM me.
- Curious and want a head start? Read `CLAUDE.md` and `README.md` to
  see the schema and pattern.

See you on workshop day.
